As your library grows, search and filter become essential for finding the right recipe quickly — whether you are building a product, choosing a recipe for an email, or just browsing.

## Full-text search

The search bar at the top of your library searches across recipe titles, descriptions, ingredient names, and notes. Search is case-insensitive and multiple terms are combined with AND logic (all terms must match).

## Filters

You can combine any of the following filters with search:

| Filter | Options |
|--------|---------|
| **Dietary tags** | Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free, Vegan, Vegetarian, Keto, Paleo, Nut-Free, Egg-Free, Soy-Free |
| **Cuisine** | Any text value (Italian, Mexican, Thai, etc.) |
| **Meal type** | Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snack, Dessert, Drink, Condiment, Side |
| **Season** | Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter, Holiday |
| **Max cook time** | Filter by maximum cook time in minutes |
| **Collection** | Filter to recipes in a specific collection |
| **Status** | Draft, Active, or Archived |
| **Email ready** | Show only recipes marked as email-ready |

## Sorting

Sort your results by:

- **Created date** (newest or oldest first)
- **Updated date** (most or least recently updated)
- **Engagement score** (highest or lowest first)
- **Title** (A-Z or Z-A)

<Aside type="tip">
  Sorting by engagement score is a great way to find your best-performing recipes when choosing content for a new product.
</Aside>

## Pagination

Search results are paginated. You can control how many recipes appear per page using the per-page selector.
