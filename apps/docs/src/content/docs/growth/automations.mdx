crumb's automation engine creates Kit sequences and broadcast drafts on your behalf, but it never sends anything without your approval. Every automation produces a draft that you review, edit, and schedule yourself.

## Save This Recipe sequences

When a subscriber clicks Save This Recipe, crumb can enroll them in a follow-up sequence. See [Save This Recipe](/email/save-this-recipe/) for full details.

## New recipe broadcast drafts

When you mark a recipe as email-ready, crumb creates a broadcast draft in Kit. See [Broadcast drafts](/email/broadcast-drafts/) for full details.

## Lead magnet delivery sequences

When you create a lead magnet, crumb generates a 4-email Kit sequence. See [Lead magnets](/products/lead-magnets/) for full details.

## Seasonal recipe drops

Seasonal drops let you schedule recipe broadcasts around a theme and timeframe.

### Setting up a seasonal drop

1. Go to **Settings** > **Automations** > **Seasonal Drops**.
2. Click **New Seasonal Drop**.
3. Configure:
   - **Season label** — for example, "Holiday Baking" or "Summer Grilling."
   - **Start date and end date** — specific dates or annual recurrence.
   - **Collection** — which collection to draw recipes from.
   - **Target segment** — all subscribers, or a specific dietary tag filter.
4. Save.

### How seasonal drops work

On the start date, crumb selects a recipe from the specified collection (choosing the highest engagement score among recipes that have not yet been used in this season's drops). It creates a Kit broadcast draft with the recipe card and notifies you in the dashboard.

You open Kit, review the draft, write your intro, and send.

<Aside>
  Seasonal drops create **one draft at a time**, not an entire series up front. This gives you the
  flexibility to skip a drop or substitute a different recipe if you want.
</Aside>

<Aside type="tip">
  Seasonal drops work best with collections of at least 8-10 recipes. This gives crumb enough
  material to rotate through without repeating.
</Aside>
