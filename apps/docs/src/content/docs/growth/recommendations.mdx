dough analyzes your segment data and recipe engagement to recommend products you should consider building. Recommendations are surfaced as notifications in your dashboard.

## How recommendations work

After each daily segment profile computation, dough checks for product opportunities. A recommendation is generated when all of the following are true:

- A dietary segment has **50 or more subscribers**.
- The engagement rate for that segment is **15% or higher** (meaning at least 15% of tagged subscribers saved a recipe in the last 30 days).
- You have **5 or more recipes** with that confirmed dietary tag.

When these conditions are met, you see a notification like:

> "200 of your subscribers engage most with gluten-free recipes. You have 12 gluten-free recipes with an average engagement score of 7.3. This could be your next ebook."

Clicking the call-to-action pre-populates the product builder with all matching recipes, sorted by engagement score.

<Aside type="tip">
  Recommendations are shown at most once per dietary tag per 30-day period. You will not be spammed
  with the same suggestion.
</Aside>

## Acting on recommendations

You do not have to follow every recommendation. Think of them as data-driven nudges. If the numbers align with what you already know about your audience, it is a strong signal to move forward.

If you are not ready to build a full ebook, consider starting with a lead magnet or recipe card pack for that dietary segment. Smaller products are faster to create and help you validate the demand before investing in a larger product.
