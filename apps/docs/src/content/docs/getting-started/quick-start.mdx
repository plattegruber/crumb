This guide walks you through the three things that matter most when you first open crumb: connecting your Kit account, importing a recipe, and inserting a recipe card into a Kit email.

## Step 1: Create your crumb account

1. Go to **app.crumb.kitchen** and click **Sign Up**.
2. Enter your email and create a password.
3. Check your inbox for the verification email and click the confirmation link.

<Aside type="tip">
  Use the same email address you use for your Kit account. This makes it easier to keep things organized.
</Aside>

## Step 2: Connect your Kit account

Once your email is verified, crumb will prompt you to connect Kit.

1. Click **Connect Kit** on your dashboard.
2. You will be redirected to Kit's authorization page.
3. Review the permissions crumb is requesting and click **Authorize**.
4. Kit redirects you back to crumb. You should see a green "Connected" badge on your dashboard.

crumb needs access to your subscribers, tags, broadcasts, sequences, forms, and webhooks to work properly. All permissions are read/write so crumb can create tags, broadcast drafts, and sequences on your behalf.

<Aside>
  crumb never sends emails without your approval. It creates drafts that you review and schedule yourself inside Kit.
</Aside>

## Step 3: Import your first recipe

Now that Kit is connected, let's get a recipe into your library.

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="From a URL">
    1. Click **Library** in the sidebar, then **Import Recipe**.
    2. Select **From URL**.
    3. Paste the URL of a recipe page from your blog or any recipe website.
    4. Click **Import**. crumb will extract the recipe title, ingredients, instructions, timing, and photos.
    5. Review the extracted recipe. Make any corrections you need.
    6. Click **Save to Library**.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="From Instagram">
    1. Click **Library** in the sidebar, then **Import Recipe**.
    2. Select **From Instagram**.
    3. Paste the URL of an Instagram post that contains a recipe in the caption.
    4. Click **Import**. crumb will extract the recipe from the caption text.
    5. Review and edit the extracted recipe, then click **Save to Library**.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="Manually">
    1. Click **Library** in the sidebar, then **New Recipe**.
    2. Fill in the recipe title, description, ingredients, and instructions.
    3. Upload a photo.
    4. Click **Save**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Aside type="tip">
  crumb automatically suggests dietary tags (like Gluten-Free or Vegan) based on your ingredients. You will see these suggestions on the recipe edit page. Confirm the ones that are accurate — only confirmed tags are used for subscriber segmentation.
</Aside>

## Step 4: Send a recipe card in Kit

With a recipe in your library, you can now insert it into a Kit email.

1. Open **Kit** and create a new broadcast or open an existing draft.
2. In the email editor, click the **+** button to add a new block.
3. Find the **Recipe Card** block (from crumb) in the block list.
4. Search for your recipe by name and select it.
5. Choose a display mode:
   - **Compact** — photo, title, cook time, and a Save button. Great for newsletters with multiple recipes.
   - **Standard** — adds a short description, dietary icons, and a partial ingredient list. The most popular choice.
   - **Full** — includes the complete ingredient list, instructions, and optional nutrition info. Best for single-recipe sends.
6. Click **Insert**. The recipe card appears in your email, styled with your brand colors and fonts.
7. Write your intro text above the card, then send or schedule the broadcast as usual.

When a subscriber clicks **Save This Recipe** in the email, crumb automatically tags them in Kit with the recipe's dietary preferences and records the engagement event. This data feeds into your analytics and segmentation over time.

## What to do next

You have the basics down. Here are some things to explore:

- **Import more recipes** — the more recipes in your library, the more useful crumb becomes. Try [bulk importing from Instagram](/recipes/importing/) or [syncing your WordPress recipes](/settings/wordpress/).
- **Set up your brand kit** — configure your colors, fonts, and logo so recipe cards match your brand. See [Brand Kit settings](/settings/brand-kit/).
- **Explore segmentation** — as subscribers interact with your recipe cards, crumb builds dietary preference segments. Learn about [how segmentation works](/growth/segmentation/).
- **Build your first product** — once you have 5 or more recipes, try creating an [ebook](/products/ebooks/) or [meal plan](/products/meal-plans/).
