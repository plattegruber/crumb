Your brand kit controls how your recipe cards and digital products look. dough uses these settings to style everything it generates — from the recipe card in a Kit email to the cover page of an ebook.

## Configuring your brand kit

Go to **Settings** > **Brand Kit** to set:

| Setting                        | What it affects                                                                                |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Primary color**              | Recipe card button backgrounds, heading text color, product accent color.                      |
| **Secondary color** (optional) | Used as a complementary color in product templates.                                            |
| **Accent color** (optional)    | Used for badges, icons, and highlights in products.                                            |
| **Heading font**               | Recipe titles in email cards and product pages. Choose any Google Font. Falls back to Georgia. |
| **Body font**                  | Ingredients, instructions, and body text. Choose any Google Font. Falls back to Arial.         |
| **Logo** (optional)            | Displayed on ebook cover pages and social share assets.                                        |

<Aside type="tip">
  Keep your primary color strong and high-contrast. It is used for buttons in emails, and buttons
  need to be easy to spot.
</Aside>

## Multiple brand kits (Studio tier)

On the Studio tier, you can create multiple brand kits. This is useful if you run different product lines, collaborate with brands, or want different visual identities for different types of content.

Each product is associated with one brand kit at creation time. Changing a product's brand kit requires re-rendering the PDF.

<Aside>
  On the Free, Creator, and Pro tiers, you have one brand kit. Upgrade to Studio for multiple brand
  kits.
</Aside>
