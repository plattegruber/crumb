Your recipe library is where all your recipes live inside crumb. It is the foundation for everything else — email recipe cards, digital products, segmentation, and analytics all start with the recipes in your library.

## What the library does

- **Stores structured recipes** with titles, descriptions, ingredients, instructions, timing, photos, dietary tags, cuisine, meal type, and season.
- **Imports recipes** from blog URLs, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, screenshots, and WordPress recipe plugins.
- **Organizes recipes** into collections that you can use to build products.
- **Searches and filters** across your entire library by text, dietary tags, cuisine, meal type, cook time, season, and more.
- **Detects duplicates** when you import a recipe that might already exist in your library.
- **Tracks engagement** by showing each recipe's engagement score alongside its details.

## Recipe lifecycle

Every recipe has a status:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Draft** | In your library but not ready for email or products. Default for newly imported recipes. |
| **Active** | Ready to insert into Kit emails and include in digital products. |
| **Archived** | Hidden from your library. Still works in existing products that reference it. |

<Aside type="tip">
  Archiving a recipe does not remove Kit tags that were already applied to subscribers. Past engagement data is preserved.
</Aside>
