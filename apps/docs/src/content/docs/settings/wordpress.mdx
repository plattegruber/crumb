If you publish recipes on a WordPress site using WP Recipe Maker or Tasty Recipes, crumb can sync those recipes into your library automatically.

## Setting up WordPress sync

1. Go to **Settings** > **WordPress**.
2. Enter your WordPress site URL.
3. Create a **WordPress Application Password**:
   - In your WordPress admin, go to **Users** > **Profile**.
   - Scroll to **Application Passwords**.
   - Enter a name (like "crumb") and click **Add New Application Password**.
   - Copy the generated password.
4. Paste the application password into crumb.
5. Click **Connect**. crumb tests the connection and detects your recipe plugin.

<Aside>
  crumb supports WP Recipe Maker and Tasty Recipes. If you use a different recipe plugin, WordPress
  sync is not available — use URL import instead.
</Aside>

## How sync works

- **New recipes** on your WordPress site are imported as Drafts in crumb.
- **Updated recipes** are refreshed in crumb — ingredients, instructions, and timing are updated. Your edits to the title, description, notes, and photos in crumb are preserved.
- **Deleted recipes** on WordPress are flagged in crumb for your review, not automatically deleted.

Sync runs on demand (click **Sync Now** in Settings) or automatically every 24 hours.

<Aside type="caution">
  If your WordPress Application Password stops working (for example, after a password reset), crumb
  will flag the sync as failed and prompt you to regenerate the password.
</Aside>
