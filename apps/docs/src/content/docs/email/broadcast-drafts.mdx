When you mark a recipe as "email ready" in your library, crumb automatically creates a broadcast draft in Kit with the recipe card pre-inserted. This saves you the repetitive work of opening Kit, creating a broadcast, and configuring the recipe card block each time.

## How it works

1. Open a recipe in your library and set its status to **Active**.
2. Toggle **Email ready** to on.
3. crumb creates a Kit broadcast draft with:
   - **Subject line:** "[Recipe Title] — a new recipe for you"
   - **Content:** A Standard-mode recipe card block, plus a blank intro text placeholder for you to fill in.
   - **Internal description:** "crumb auto-draft — [Recipe Title]"
4. You receive a notification in crumb: "Your Kit draft is ready — [Recipe Title]."
5. Open Kit, find the draft, write your intro text, and send or schedule as usual.

<Aside>
  crumb never sends emails on your behalf. Broadcast drafts are always created as drafts in Kit. You review, edit, and schedule them yourself.
</Aside>

## Customizing drafts

The auto-generated draft is a starting point. You can:

- Change the subject line.
- Write your intro story or note above the recipe card.
- Change the recipe card's display mode (click the card block in Kit's editor).
- Add other content blocks above or below the card.
- Target the broadcast to a specific segment using Kit's subscriber filters.

<Aside type="tip">
  For the best engagement, write a short personal intro above the recipe card. Share the story behind the recipe, a tip for making it, or why you love it.
</Aside>
